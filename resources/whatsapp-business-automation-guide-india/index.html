The WhatsApp Business Automation Guide for India

By Reckona AIUpdated 20 July 202612 min read

In India, WhatsApp isn't a channel — it's the channel. Your customers already live there, reply there, and pay attention there in a way they never will on email. This guide covers how to automate it properly: the setup that scales, the compliance rules that keep your number alive, and the twelve flows that actually make money.

Business App vs Business API

Rule of thumb: if you're reading a guide about automation, you need the API. Access comes through a Business Solution Provider (BSP); setup involves Meta Business verification, a dedicated number, display-name approval, and message-template approval. Budget a week for the paperwork.

Compliance — the part that keeps your number alive

  • Opt-in is mandatory for business-initiated messages. Collect it explicitly (checkout, forms, ads, QR) and store proof.
  • Templates — anything you send outside a 24-hour customer-service window must be a pre-approved template.
  • Quality rating is your licence. Block rates drive rating; rating drives your daily send cap. One spammy campaign can throttle the whole number.
  • Always offer opt-out, honour it instantly, and never buy contact lists — that's how numbers die.

The 12 revenue flows

  1. Lead-capture instant response — new enquiry answered in seconds with qualification questions. Speed-to-lead is the single highest-leverage number in sales.
  2. Lead qualification bot — budget, need, timeline collected before a human joins; CRM updated automatically.
  3. Abandoned enquiry revival — polite nudge at 24h and 72h to leads that went quiet. Routinely revives 10–20%.
  4. Quote follow-up — every quote chased on a schedule until answered. Nothing dies in an inbox.
  5. Appointment/site-visit scheduler — booking, reminders, reschedule handling. No-shows drop sharply with a morning-of reminder.
  6. Order/status updates — proactive dispatch and delivery notifications that pre-empt the "where is my order" call.
  7. Payment reminders — due-date sequences with payment links. Usually pays for the whole system by itself.
  8. FAQ & support deflection — a bot answering from your own documents, with clean human handoff. See our chatbot work.
  9. Feedback & review engine — post-delivery rating ask; happy customers routed to Google reviews.
  10. Re-order/renewal nudges — consumables and subscriptions prompted at the natural interval.
  11. Broadcast campaigns — segmented, template-approved offers to opted-in lists. 90%+ open rates when quality rating is healthy.
  12. Internal alerts — order, payment and exception alerts to your own team's groups, straight from your systems.

Benchmarks from well-run flows: 90%+ open rates, 30–60% response rates, and reminder flows converting at several times email. WhatsApp underperforms only when it's treated like an email blaster — which is also how numbers get blocked.

Build order

Start with flows 1, 4 and 7 — instant response, quote follow-up, payment reminders. They touch revenue directly, need no content production, and prove ROI inside a month. Layer the bot (flow 8) once volume justifies it. For the wider automation picture, see what manual ops really cost an SME.
