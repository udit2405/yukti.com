How to get cited by ChatGPT (and Gemini, Perplexity): a 2026 checklist

By Reckona AIUpdated 24 July 20268 min read

When a buyer asks an AI engine a question, it answers with a synthesized paragraph that names two or three sources. Getting your brand into that shortlist — cited, recommended, and described correctly — is the single highest-leverage move in AI-era search. Here's the checklist we run for clients.

Why citations are the new prize

On a classic results page, being ranked #4 still earns clicks. In an AI answer, being the fourth-best source earns nothing — the engine quotes two or three and moves on. So the goal shifts from ranking to being retrieved and named. That depends far less on any single keyword and far more on how legible, quotable and corroborated your brand is across the web.

The six signals AI engines look for

SignalWhat it meansHow to build it
Entity clarityThe engine knows who you are as a distinct thingConsistent name, category and facts across your site, schema and the web
Quotable structureContent shaped in clean, liftable chunksDirect-answer paragraphs, definitions, short lists, clear headings
Citable dataOriginal facts worth quotingPublish your own research, benchmarks, prices, methods
CorroborationOther sources say the same thing about youDirectories, industry publications, reviews AI engines crawl
FreshnessRecently updated, dated contentVisible publish/update dates, periodic refreshes
CrawlabilityThe engine can actually read the pageServer-rendered content, no accidental AI-crawler blocks, schema

The order to fix them

  • Start with crawlability and entity clarity. If AI crawlers can't read you, or can't tell what you are, nothing else matters. Server-render key pages, add Organization and FAQ schema, and make your name, category and location consistent everywhere.
  • Then make content quotable. Lead sections with a direct one-sentence answer, then support it. Engines lift clean sentences, not buried ones.
  • Then publish citable data. One original benchmark or study in your category does more than ten generic blog posts — it gives engines a reason to name you specifically.
  • Then earn corroboration. Get listed and quoted on the third-party sources AI engines already trust in your sector.

The one-line rule: write the sentence you'd want ChatGPT to quote about you — then make sure it's on your site, backed by data, and repeated by sources the engine trusts.

How to know it's working

You can't improve what you don't measure. Baseline a prompt panel: run 30–40 real buyer questions across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude and Perplexity, and score whether you're mentioned, recommended and described accurately. Re-run it monthly. That's exactly what our GEO practice does, and the method behind our study of 50 Indian brands — where 34 were invisible.
