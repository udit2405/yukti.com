AI appointment automation for clinics and hospitals

By Reckona AIUpdated 24 July 20268 min read

In most clinics, the front desk spends its day on the phone — booking, rescheduling, reminding, chasing reports. It's the least clinical work in the building and the easiest to automate. Here's how to do it without losing the human touch patients expect.

The front-desk bottleneck

Appointment calls, WhatsApp messages and walk-in queries all land on the same one or two people. The result: long hold times, missed after-hours enquiries, and staff too buried in logistics to handle patients well. No-shows quietly drain the OPD calendar on top of it.

What to automate

  • Booking. A WhatsApp flow that shows real availability and books directly — 24/7, no call needed.
  • Reminders. Automated confirmations and reminders that measurably cut no-shows.
  • Reports & follow-up. Test reports and post-visit follow-ups delivered automatically, with a human handoff for anything clinical.
  • Insurance paperwork. Pre-authorisation and claim documents extracted and routed instead of typed.
TaskAutomate?
Booking, rescheduling, remindersYes — fully
Report delivery & routine follow-upYes — with human handoff
Insurance document processingYes — with a review step
Clinical advice & triageNo — keep human

Keep the human where it counts

Automation should absorb logistics, not medicine. The rule: automate the scheduling and paperwork; route anything involving clinical judgment to a person immediately. Done right, staff spend their time on patients, not the phone.

Fastest win: a WhatsApp booking-and-reminder flow. It cuts no-shows and after-hours misses in weeks, and it's where most clinics should start.
