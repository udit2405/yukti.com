GEO · Pillar guide

The Complete Guide to Generative Engine Optimization (2026)

By Reckona AIUpdated 20 July 202614 min read

Your customers now ask ChatGPT, Gemini and Perplexity before they ask Google. When they do, the engine answers with three or four brand names — not ten blue links. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the discipline of becoming one of those names.

What GEO actually is

GEO is the practice of making your brand retrievable, correctly described, and recommended inside AI answer engines. Where SEO earns you a position on a results page, GEO earns you a citation inside the answer itself. The distinction matters because an AI answer is a zero-sum recommendation: if the engine names your competitor and not you, there is no "page two" for the buyer to scroll to.

How AI engines choose which brands to recommend

Every major engine assembles answers from three layers, and each layer is optimizable:

  1. Model memory. What the model learned about your category during training — dominated by high-authority, frequently-crawled sources: Wikipedia-grade references, trade publications, established directories, widely-syndicated data.
  2. Live retrieval. ChatGPT Search, Perplexity and AI Overviews fetch pages at answer time. Content that is structured, quotable and directly answers the question gets pulled in; long unstructured brochures do not.
  3. Entity graph. Engines resolve brands into entities. If your company's name, category, location and offerings are inconsistent across the web — or absent from the sources engines trust — you are ambiguous, and ambiguous brands don't get recommended.

The six levers of GEO

1. Entity clarity

One canonical name, one clear category sentence ("X is a Y that does Z for A"), used identically on your site, LinkedIn, directories, and every citation-worthy page. Add Organization and Service schema so the claim is machine-readable.

2. Citable data assets

Engines love to cite numbers. Original benchmarks, price surveys, industry studies and calculators earn citations that plain service pages never will. One good data study outperforms fifty blog posts.

3. Answer-shaped content

Pages structured as direct answers: a question as the H2, a two-sentence answer immediately below, evidence after. FAQ schema on top. This is what live retrieval quotes.

4. Third-party corroboration

Models trust what multiple independent sources agree on. Digital PR into AI-crawled trade media, expert commentary, and listings in the comparison articles engines already cite for your category.

5. Technical retrievability

Fast, clean HTML; no content locked behind JavaScript that crawlers skip; sitemaps; and crucially — do not block AI crawlers (GPTBot, Google-Extended, PerplexityBot) in robots.txt unless you've made that trade deliberately.

6. Measurement

Fix a panel of 30–50 buyer-intent prompts ("best category supplier in India", "compare X vs Y"…). Run it monthly across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and AI Overviews. Score mentions, citations and accuracy. That share-of-voice number is your GEO KPI.

Frequently asked questions

Is GEO replacing SEO?

No — it compounds on top of it. Engines retrieve from the same web SEO built. But budgets are splitting: rankings and retrievability are now different games.

How long until results?

Retrieval-layer wins (being quoted by ChatGPT Search or Perplexity) can land in weeks. Model-memory wins compound over quarters. That's why early movers keep the advantage.

Does this work for Indian mid-market companies?

Especially well — most Indian categories have near-zero GEO competition today. We tested 50 Indian brands; 34 were invisible.
